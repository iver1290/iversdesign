About 

Luke Ivers is an architect and educator with extensive experience in design, project leadership, and creative exploration. He is the founder of Ivers Design, an independent practice launched in 2017, and has worked in senior roles at firms including ELS Architecture & Urban Design, SGPA Architecture, Hummel Architects, and W|A Architects & Engineers. His portfolio includes award-winning projects across typologies such as civic, commercial, mixed-use, and educational design.

Luke has also served as an adjunct professor, teaching architecture at the University of Idaho and Diablo Valley College, where he mentored students in design thinking, representation, and studio processes. His work integrates technical proficiency with a deep interest in experiential design, often exploring themes of spatial narrative, atmosphere, and sensory engagement.

Proficient in digital tools like Rhinoceros, Revit, SketchUp, Lumion, and Adobe Creative Suite, Luke combines digital craft with conceptual clarity. His current interests lie at the intersection of architecture, well-being, and personal mastery—shaped by both professional practice and lived experience.
